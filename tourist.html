Sangiran Museum

Sangiran Museum (ꦩꦸꦱꦶꦪꦸꦩ꧀ꦩꦤꦸꦁꦱꦥꦸꦂꦮꦱꦔꦶꦫꦤ꧀) is an archaeological excavation site in Java, Indonesia. According to a UNESCO report (1995), "Sangiran is recognized by scientists to be one of the most important sites in the world for studying fossil man, ranking alongside Zhoukoudian (China), Willandra Lakes (Australia), Olduvai Gorge (Tanzania), and Sterkfontein (South Africa), and more fruitful in finds than any of these."

Sangiran Cluster Ngebung Museum

The Sangiran Cluster Ngebung Museum is located in Ngebung Village, Kalijambe District, Sragen Regency. The ngebung site has invaluable historical value because it is the location where ancient human fossils were first systematically excavated with amazing results. The discoveries at the Ngebung site include animal fossils, artifacts and remains of past human life. There are many things you can learn when visiting the Ngebung cluster.

Sangiran Cluster Bukuran Museum

The Sangiran Cluster Bukuran Museum is located in the Bukuran area, Kalijambe District and is one of the supporting museums of the Sangiran Museum which is rich in human fossil remains. Most of the remains of the ancient human species Homo erectus from Sangiran were found at this site. The lower floor of the Bukuran Cluster Museum displays evidence of ancient human fossil remains (replicas) that have ever been found. Not only fossils come from the Sangiran Site, but also ancient human fossils from paleoanthropological sites throughout the world. Around 12 casts of human skull fossils are presented so that visitors can recognize and compare the stages of human physical development.

Bayanan Hot Springs

This tourist attraction is located in Bayanan Village, Jambeyan, Sambirejo. The water in this place is called God water or Hyang Tirto which is believed to be able to cure various diseases, such as rheumatism, itching, etc. Many visitors are curious and want to prove for themselves the benefits of the water here. Located in a beautiful forest environment, with fresh air located on the northern slopes of Mount Lawu.

Kedung Grujug

The name Kedung Grujug does sound foreign to the ear. Even the mini waterfall located between Doyong Village and Soko Village in Miri District, Sragen Regency is still empty of visitors and hidden. Traced from its name, Kedung Grujug comes from Javanese. Kedung means deep river, and Grujug means flush. Not surprisingly, this waterfall shower with a height of around 5 meters produces a chorus of gemrujug, aka gurgling sounds. Suitable for travelers who want to find peace and relieve fatigue.

Betisrejo Nature Tourism

Betisrejo Nature Tourism in Jambeyan Village, Sambirejo District, Sragen Regency was developed to preserve the village's naturalness and lifestyle to return to nature, making three villages in Sambirejo District, namely Jambeyan, Jetis and Sukorejo (abbreviated Betisrejo) designated as community-based Organic Tourism Villages. able to attract domestic and foreign tourists. Supporting facilities for this natural tourism are Gebyar Reservoir, Kyai Srenggi Camping Ground, Mount Tunggangan Wildlife Park, Banyanan and Ngunut Hot Springs Nature Tourism.

Dung Jengglong Waterfall

Dung Jengglong is an alternative water tourism in Sragen. Dung Jengglong, that's what people call it. Located in the middle of the Jengglong River, Dukuh Mojolegi, Bagor Village, Dung Jengglong offers the sensation of playing in the water amidst limestone rocks. Not only that, this new tourist spot also has a three meter high mini waterfall. The river basin at the point where the water falls is not that deep. Surrounded by bamboo and teak trees, Dung Jengglong feels shady during the day. The blue water seems to invite anyone to be swept away by its coldness.

Gunung Banyak

The Gunung Banyak tourist destination in Srawung, Sragen, Central Java is one of the tourist attractions in Srawung village, Sragen district, Central Java province, Indonesia. The Gunung Banyak tourist destination in Srawung Sragen, Central Java is a tourist spot that is busy with tourists on weekdays and holidays. This place is very beautiful and can provide a different sensation to our daily activities. The Gunung Banyak tourist destination in Srawung Sragen, Central Java has a charming beauty that is very interesting to visit. It would be a shame if you were in the city of Sragen and did not visit this natural tourist attraction which has unparalleled beauty.
