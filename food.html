Local Food from Sragen

Intip (Frying Rice Crust)

This typical food is definitely familiar, especially as a typical Sragen souvenir. Its crunchy texture and savory taste combined with the richness of melted palm sugar make peepe one of the Sragen culinary delights that is a shame to miss. Intip (Frying Rice Crust) is a rice crust that is molded in a distinctive round shape, similar to a saucepan. Even though the texture is similar to rengginang, this snack has a different taste and is always delicious as a snack.

Dawet

When it is hot and sunny, dawet is the most delicious food to eat. This Sragen culinary dish is made from rice flour which is served with grated ice, brown sugar and other accompaniments. With a sweet, savory taste plus a refreshing sensation, it's no wonder that dawet is one of the culinary delights that many travelers look for while in Sragen.

Botok Mercon

Different from ordinary Botok which is mainly made from river tamarind and grated coconut, this Botok is actually a development of Garang Asem (a food with a spicy and sour taste with fish or chicken as a side dish). Because of its super spicy taste, tourists call it Botok Mercon (Mercon: Firecracker) since it tastes like it burns in your mouth when you eat it.

Pecel Tumpang

Pecel Tumpang is a traditional food that is sought after by culinary lovers in various regions. One of them is a typical food from Sragen Regency, namely overlapping pecel. The uniqueness of this dish is that it uses the main ingredients, namely tempeh that is about to rot tempeh (semangit) and tempeh that is already cooked. Maybe for those of you who are foreign it will sound a little strange and think why can tempeh that is about to rot be used as the main ingredient in a food, but don't ever think about it being strange if you haven't enjoyed it directly.

Sego Plontang

Sego plontang is a typical Javanese food, more specifically a typical Sragen food. This food contains several types of dishes, namely nasi uduk (uduk rice) or Javanese people usually call it sego gurih (savory rice), shredded chicken, fried black soybeans, wader (yellow rasbora) fish, peanuts and crackers. Sego plontang is always used in every Javanese ritual, such as suroan, which is a ritual carried out by Javanese people to thank God Almighty for harmony and unity in society and also to commemorate local customs carried out in the month of Muharram.

Emping Garut

Emping garut has its own charm amidst various types of modern food today. The processing method is simple. Arrowroot tubers that have been peeled, washed and then boiled. Once cooked, cut into 2 cm pieces, then pound until flat. After that, it is dried in the sun, then seasoned and fried. Crispy and delicious arrowroot chips are ready to be enjoyed.

Soto Girin

Sragen apparently has a series of legendary delicious culinary delights, one of which is Soto Girin. No doubt, this soto is a legendary one in Sragen. This soto is a representation of typical Sragen soto. While Soto Boyolali and Soto Solo soup tends to be clear in color, Soto Girin's soup is cloudy or buthek (murky) because it is rich in spices. This soto feels very special because it is cooked in a clay pot with wood burning in the fire. The taste of this delicious soto broth is guaranteed to make you addicted. A portion of Soto Girin contains rice, pieces of beef, sprouts, fried onions and sliced celery.
